ACT IV, SCENE III

*A sparse room. LEON stands at the window, his back to the door. MARCUS enters.*

**MARCUS:**
I know what you must think. Don't answer yet.
I sold you out because I was afraid,
and fear is not a thing I can defend.
I'm asking for a word. Just — hear me out.

**LEON:**
You walked into my house. I let you in.
That's more than you deserve. So — speak your piece.

**MARCUS:**
I told them where you'd be. I gave your name.
That's all I gave — the rest was theirs to do.
I drew a line. I thought the line would hold.
I thought, if I gave less, they'd ask no more.

**LEON:**
You never meant. That's very like you, Marcus.
Intent without the deed — your whole career.
And yet here I still stand and breathe and speak —
you wonder why I haven't broken yet?

**MARCUS:**
I came because you're all I have. Because
I've spent six weeks inside the dark of this,
and I need you to tell me — is there room?
Some version where you let me back from here?

**LEON:**
*(a long pause)*
Sit down.

**MARCUS:**
What?

**LEON:**
Sit. I want to look at you.

*(MARCUS sits. LEON turns from the window at last.)*

You want forgiveness. What a tender word.
It costs the speaker nothing — heals them whole —
and leaves the one who hears it holding still
the weight the speaker dropped. I've thought of this.
I've thought of you at three a.m. each night,
rehearsing what I'd say if you came back.
I had a speech. It burned a hole right through me.

**MARCUS:**
Then say it. Whatever it costs — I'll hear it.

**LEON:**
Here's what I have instead of that old speech:
The king has signed your warrant. Yesterday.
They needed someone new to sell, and you —
you told them everything, which means you know
too much for them to keep you walking free.
I heard this morning. Thought you ought to know.
Not from a guard. From something like a friend.

**MARCUS:**
*(standing slowly)*
You're saying — *(stops)* — you held it back. As I —

**LEON:**
                                                            Yes.
They will come for you at dawn.
I couldn't stop it. And I didn't try —
not for a while. I sat here in this room
and let the clock run down before I called.
An hour remaining. Marcus — maybe less.

**MARCUS:**
So all of this — my coming here, my speech —

**LEON:**
Was real. I think it was. And far too late.
I do forgive you. There. Now take it. Go.

# The Tides of Saltmarrow

**Setting:** Saltmarrow, a fishing village of ~400 on the Cerulean Coast. The docks smell of rot and low tide. Nets hang empty. Children aren't allowed near the water.

---

## The Situation

For seven days, fishermen have hauled up only bones — human and animal, picked white, reeking faintly of copper and brine. Three days ago, **Mayor Aldric Voss** (58, heavyset, desperate) vanished. Yesterday, fisherman **Torben Ash** staggered into the **Broken Anchor** tavern muttering about *"singing in the deep."*

**Harbormaster Mira Canto** (pragmatic, sleep-deprived, personally losing money) hires the party for 400gp to find Voss and stop whatever is killing the fish. **High tide is in six hours.** After that, she says, she's not sure the town will still be here to pay them.

---

## What's Really Happening

Beneath the bay lie the ruins of **Deepholm**, a drowned temple to Umberlee, sealed 200 years ago. Inside, the undead cultist **Seraveth** has been slowly awakening. Mayor Voss, facing bankruptcy and loan sharks from Velmoor, found a ritual tablet in his cellar and made a bargain — "the bounty of the bay" in exchange for the town's debts erased. He didn't understand he was offering Seraveth the life-force of every living thing in the water. Now he's chained to the altar inside Deepholm, alive but fading, while Seraveth completes a ritual to raise a drowned army. It finishes at high tide.

---

## Three Paths In

**Path A — Follow the Mayor:** Voss's assistant, **Nella Bright** (nervous, guilty — she's the one who found him the tablet), can be persuaded or pressured into revealing that Voss rowed out alone three nights ago to coordinates marked on his desk map. She'll resist unless the party makes her understand people will die.

**Path B — Follow the Bones:** Torben Ash, once sobered (DC 12 Persuasion or a flask of something strong), describes a cold, copper-smelling calm patch about a mile out. He'll row them there and has a crude diving barrel he built himself. DC 14 Nature or Survival identifies it as the roof of a submerged structure.

**Path C — The Tide-Reader:** **Oswen**, a blind 70-year-old who reads patterns in tide wrack, recognized this morning's bone formation as a summoning diagram. She knows Deepholm's history and Seraveth's one weakness — a seal glyph carved above the temple door that, if invoked, prevents Seraveth from detonating when killed. She'll share it for one favor: retrieve her drowned son's ring from the ruins.

---

## Deepholm (Three Encounters, Rising Water Throughout)

**Entry Hall** — knee-deep and rising; 5 skeletons, DC 13 Athletics to move freely as pressure builds.

**The Choir Chamber** — four drowned cultists chanting; silencing all four stops the flood. Stopping only some accelerates it.

**Seraveth's Sanctum** — Voss chained to the altar, barely conscious. Seraveth fights in deep water with darkness and a grapple-and-drown attack. Without Oswen's glyph, killing her triggers a death burst: DC 15 Con, 4d10 necrotic.

---

## Consequences Matter

- **Save Voss + seal the temple:** Town survives. Party decides whether to expose Voss's deal publicly — Nella begs them not to.
- **Seal without saving Voss:** Temple locked, Voss dies inside. Mira pays, but Nella never forgives them.
- **Save Voss, don't seal:** Army reduced to stragglers — but Seraveth reforms in thirty days.
- **Fail the timer:** Twelve drowned warriors rise from the bay. Final battle on the docks at dawn, town burning behind them.

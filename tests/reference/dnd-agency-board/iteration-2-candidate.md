# The Bones of Saltmire

## One-Shot Adventure for 4 Level-5 Characters (3-4 Hours)

### Setup

Saltmire clings to a chalk cliff on the Straaden Coast. For seven days, every net has surfaced tangled with humanoid bones. Yesterday, Mayor **Elda Thresh** vanished -- door locked from inside, salt water on the floor, claw marks gouging the windowsill. Her daughter **Maren Thresh** (lobster-boat captain, pragmatic, terrified) hires the party: 200 gp and Elda's *cloak of the manta ray* if they bring her back alive. Elda dies at high tide -- six hours from arrival.

### The Interruption

Roughly 90 minutes in, regardless of thread, a **drowned one** (wight stats) erupts from the harbor and drags **Tomas**, a 12-year-old net-mender, underwater in full view of the party. They must choose: dive in now (DC 14 Athletics, fight 1 wight in water, disadvantage on non-aquatic attacks) or stay on task and hear Tomas's mother screaming his name for the rest of the session. Saving Tomas costs 30+ minutes of the clock. Failing to act makes Maren and all townsfolk refuse further help.

### Three Threads, Three Climaxes

**Thread A -- The Sea Caves.** Fisherman **Corvin Blackweed** (gruff, three-fingered, drinks at the Keel & Cork) saw something dragging a body into the water. He talks for 10 gp or DC 13 Persuasion. He points to **flooded sea caves** accessible only at low tide (4 hours from arrival). Inside: a coral-cage shrine to **The Unraveler**, tended by 3 drowned ones. **Climax here:** storm the shrine, kill the guardians (CR 5-6), free Elda before the tide returns and drowns everyone. Pure combat rescue under a flooding-room timer.

**Thread B -- The Tidespeaker Chapel.** Investigation at Elda's house (DC 14) uncovers a ledger: she paid someone called "Brother Silt" monthly for two years. The trail leads to a shuttered cliffside church. **Brother Silt** (merfolk warlock, believes he is saving the town) has been converting townsfolk into amphibious servants. He offers a deal: water-breathing in exchange for loyalty. He admits Elda came willingly but tried to leave. **Climax here:** Silt will release Elda if the party submits to a binding ritual (permanent water-breathing but Silt can compel obedience once), or they fight him in his chapel (Silt + 2 converted townsfolk, CR 6). Negotiation (DC 16) can halve his forces if the party turns converts against him.

**Thread C -- The Bone Tomb.** DC 13 Medicine/Arcana on the dock bones reveals they are centuries old, recently disturbed. DC 15 Nature identifies deep-trench upwelling. Maren's boat reaches a **collapsed underwater tomb** one mile out where **Vorrow**, a sea hag, dredges corpses to build a bone golem (flesh golem stats, reskinned). **Climax here:** Vorrow offers Elda's location for Brother Silt's literal heart. Accept and she teleports the party to Silt for an assassination -- or refuse, and fight Vorrow plus the half-finished golem in her tomb (CR 6-7), finding a scrying pool that reveals Elda's exact location.

### Faction Fallout

Whichever thread resolves the rescue, the surviving faction retaliates within the hour. Destroyed the shrine? Vorrow sends bone constructs into town. Killed Silt? His converts sabotage the docks. Killed Vorrow? The bone tide intensifies. The party must handle one final 2-round skirmish (CR 4) to secure Saltmire -- or bargain with the surviving power at a terrible price.

### Key NPCs

| NPC | Motivation | Betrays party if... |
|---|---|---|
| Maren Thresh | Save her mother | Party ignores Tomas or abandons search |
| Brother Silt | Convert Saltmire to serve The Unraveler | Party sides with Vorrow |
| Vorrow (sea hag) | Build golem army, destroy Silt | Party sides with Silt or destroys bones |
| Corvin Blackweed | Survive, drink, be left alone | Threatened or cheated |

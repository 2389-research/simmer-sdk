# The Sands of Sipping Well

## One-Shot Adventure — 4 Players, Level 5, 3-4 Hours

### Hook

For three days the oasis town of **Sipping Well** has been dying. Each dawn, the spring-fed pools at the town's heart shudder, cloud over, and disgorge wet sand where clean water should flow. By mid-morning the pools refill — but the yield shrinks. Yesterday the pools gave only a quarter of their usual volume. Tomorrow, the elders say, there may be nothing at all.

The party arrives at dusk to find **Councilwoman Tavia Renk** — a stern, sun-scarred woman with ink-stained ledgers — offering 800 gold and lifetime water-rights to anyone who can stop it. She shares three facts:

1. **Zahreen**, the town healer and herbalist, vanished three nights ago — the same night the corruption began. Her sandstone clinic on the east bluff is sealed shut and her apprentice, **Ludo** (a nervous half-elf teenager), hasn't been inside since he heard "grinding sounds" from below.
2. A prospector named **Orin Blacktallow** arrived a tenday ago, asking questions about an old shrine beneath the town. He's been seen entering the **Bleached Gulch** canyon north of the oasis at night.
3. The town's buried aqueduct — a relic of an ancient empire — runs from the shrine under the oasis northward toward the gulch. Nobody has walked it in living memory.

### Three Paths

**Path A — The Clinic.** Breaking into Zahreen's clinic reveals her ransacked notes on the shrine and a trapdoor to the ancient aqueduct. Inside: a trapped corridor (DC 14 Perception / DC 13 Thieves' Tools), two **sand mephits** feeding on residual magic, and evidence Zahreen went north through the tunnel willingly — her bootprints overlay Orin's. This path is quieter but longer — the aqueduct is a half-mile crawl through low, dripping stone, emerging at the shrine from below via a grate in the floor.

**Path B — The Gulch.** Tracking Orin (DC 12 Survival) into Bleached Gulch leads to a hidden canyon camp: a bedroll, a half-empty bottle of **Sandtongue Whiskey**, and a journal describing the **Eidolon of Thirst** — a bound water elemental trapped beneath the shrine whose essence Orin plans to siphon into a portable decanter worth a fortune. Two **bandits** and a **dust devil** (reskinned air elemental, CR 2) guard the canyon mouth. After defeating them, the party finds a short passage directly into the shrine's upper gallery — the fastest route.

**Path C — Diplomacy.** Persuading Ludo (DC 13 Persuasion) or Tavia (DC 15) reveals that Zahreen had been secretly maintaining the shrine's wards for years. She likely went to confront Orin herself. This path gives the party a **ward-token** (one-use: advantage on saves vs. the Eidolon's abilities) and a shortcut through the aqueduct. The ward-token also tells the party which binding sigils to target — this knowledge matters at the shrine.

### Mid-Point Escalation: The Pools Fail Early

Regardless of path chosen, when the party is roughly halfway to the shrine, **the pools fail ahead of schedule**. Read or paraphrase:

> A deep groan rolls through the ground beneath your feet. Behind you, back toward town, screams. A child's voice, high and panicked: *"The water — it's all sand!"* The air goes dry and hot, and you feel grit coating the inside of your throat. Whatever Orin is doing down there, he's accelerating. You don't have until dawn. You have hours.

**Mechanical effect:** The party must reach the shrine within 1 hour of in-game time. Long rests are impossible. Short rests cost 30 minutes. Any remaining encounters on their path gain a **time pressure rider**: the DM rolls a d6 after each combat round. On a 1, the ground shudders and everyone makes a DC 12 Dex save or falls prone as sand geysers erupt through cracks in the floor.

### The Shrine (Climax)

**The Sunken Shrine of Adath-Ka** is a domed chamber forty feet across and thirty feet high, carved from pale sandstone veined with lapis lazuli. Wave-patterns spiral across the walls and converge on a circular depression at the center — the **Binding Pool**, ten feet in diameter, now boiling with alternating surges of black water and powdered sand. Four stone pillars ring the pool, each inscribed with a **binding sigil** that pulses faintly blue. The air tastes of copper and ozone. The temperature swings wildly — frigid near the pool, furnace-hot at the walls. A low, atonal hum vibrates in the party's teeth and sternum.

**Three zones:**
- **The Binding Pool (center):** Difficult terrain. Creatures starting their turn here take 1d6 cold damage. The Eidolon has half cover while partially submerged.
- **The Pillar Ring (15 ft. radius):** The four pillars provide half cover. Destroying a sigil-pillar (AC 15, 25 HP) weakens the Eidolon (disadvantage on attacks for 1 round) but also brings the Eidolon closer to full release.
- **The Gallery (outer edge and upper ledge, 5 ft. above):** Path B enters here. Ranged attackers on the ledge have high ground. A collapsed section in the northeast is choked with rubble (difficult terrain).

**Orin Blacktallow** stands at the north edge of the pool, one hand plunged into the churning water, the other clutching a crystalline decanter that glows with stolen light. He is a gaunt man in his fifties with sunken cheeks, a patchy grey beard, and fingernails blackened by alchemical burns. His leather coat is slick with condensation. His eyes are wide — not with madness, but with desperate greed.

When he sees the party, he does not attack immediately. He speaks:

> *"Don't. You don't understand what this is. There's enough power in this thing to irrigate a hundred towns — but only if I pull it free. Zahreen wanted to keep it locked down here, serving one dusty village forever. I'm offering the world a river."*

If pressed or threatened, his voice hardens:

> *"I'm not the villain here. I've been poor my entire life. You know what a Decanter of Endless Water sells for? Enough to buy a town. I will not walk away from this."*

**Orin** uses the **cult fanatic** stat block (CR 2) with one modification: he can use a bonus action to channel the decanter, forcing one creature within 30 ft. to make a DC 14 Strength save or be pushed 10 ft. by a jet of pressurized water.

**Zahreen** is alive but encased in crystallized sand against the southwest pillar, her face locked in a grimace (DC 15 Athletics or 20 HP to free). She can re-seal the Eidolon if freed — but only if the party holds off both Orin and the Eidolon for **3 rounds** while she completes the ritual.

**The Eidolon of Thirst** uses the **water elemental** stat block with the following **Lair Action** (initiative 20, loses ties): all water within 30 ft. turns to abrasive sand for one round, dealing 2d6 bludgeoning to creatures standing in it (DC 14 Dex save for half). The sand-pulse also extinguishes non-magical flames and coats weapons, imposing disadvantage on the next attack roll made with an affected weapon.

#### How Path Choice Shapes the Climax

**Path B (Gulch — fastest route):** The party arrives via the upper gallery while the unbinding is still in progress. **Orin is vulnerable** — he must keep one hand in the pool to sustain the siphon, meaning he cannot move or use somatic spell components until he withdraws (losing 1 round of action). The Eidolon is **partially free**: it has half its maximum HP (57 HP instead of 114), cannot use its Lair Action for the first 2 rounds, and is anchored within 15 ft. of the pool. The party can ambush or parley from the high ground.

**Path A (Clinic — slowest route):** The party arrives via the floor grate, emerging inside the pillar ring with no elevation advantage. By this time, **Orin has finished the siphon** and stands free with the charged decanter. The Eidolon is **fully unbound**: full HP (114), full Lair Actions from round 1, and free to move anywhere in the shrine. Orin fights freely. Zahreen's crystallized prison has hardened further (DC 17 Athletics or 30 HP to free, instead of DC 15 / 20 HP).

**Path C (Diplomacy — moderate speed, ward-token):** The party arrives at roughly the same time as Path A (via the aqueduct), but the **ward-token** provides advantage on saves vs. the Eidolon's abilities and, crucially, the party knows which pillar holds the **master sigil** (the northeast one). Destroying only that pillar (AC 15, 25 HP) stuns the Eidolon for 1 full round without weakening the remaining bindings — a tactical opening that other paths don't have.

### Decision Point at the Climax

- **Seal the Eidolon** (free Zahreen, protect her for 3 rounds): Town saved, Zahreen grateful, full reward. Orin can be captured or driven off.
- **Destroy the Eidolon** (reduce to 0 HP): Water returns but the shrine collapses — the town has maybe five years before the oasis fails permanently. Orin escapes in the chaos.
- **Side with Orin** (help siphon the Eidolon): Party gains a **Decanter of Endless Water** but Sipping Well is doomed within a week. Zahreen becomes a recurring enemy.
- **Negotiate** (DC 18 Persuasion, only possible if Orin hasn't been attacked): Orin agrees to take a partial siphon — the Decanter works but produces only a trickle. Sipping Well survives with reduced flow. Nobody fully wins. Zahreen is disappointed but alive.

### Consequences

If the party seals the Eidolon, dawn breaks with the pools running clear. Tavia pays in full and names them **Wardens of the Well** — a title that grants them free lodging and information in any desert settlement that trades with Sipping Well.

If the party negotiated with Orin, the town survives on rationed water. Tavia pays half the reward and asks the party to find an alternative water source — a possible sequel hook. Orin leaves peacefully but may resurface as an ally or obstacle.

# The Sands of Sipping Well

## One-Shot Adventure — 4 Players, Level 5, 3-4 Hours

### Hook

For three days the oasis town of **Sipping Well** has been dying. Each dawn, the spring-fed pools at the town's heart shudder, cloud over, and disgorge wet sand where clean water should flow. By mid-morning the pools refill — but the yield shrinks. Yesterday the pools gave only a quarter of their usual volume. Tomorrow, the elders say, there may be nothing at all.

The party arrives at dusk to find **Councilwoman Tavia Renk** — a stern, sun-scarred woman with ink-stained ledgers — offering 800 gold and lifetime water-rights to anyone who can stop it. She shares three facts:

1. **Zahreen**, the town healer and herbalist, vanished three nights ago — the same night the corruption began. Her sandstone clinic on the east bluff is sealed shut and her apprentice, **Ludo** (a nervous half-elf teenager), hasn't been inside since he heard "grinding sounds" from below.
2. A prospector named **Orin Blacktallow** arrived a tenday ago, asking questions about an old shrine beneath the town. He's been seen entering the **Bleached Gulch** canyon north of the oasis at night.
3. The town's buried aqueduct — a relic of an ancient empire — runs from the shrine under the oasis northward toward the gulch. Nobody has walked it in living memory.

### Three Paths

**Path A — The Clinic.** Breaking into Zahreen's clinic reveals her ransacked notes on the shrine and a trapdoor to the ancient aqueduct. Inside: a trapped corridor (DC 14 Perception / DC 13 Thieves' Tools), two **sand mephits** feeding on residual magic, and evidence Zahreen went north through the tunnel willingly — her bootprints overlay Orin's. This path is quieter but longer, emerging at the shrine from below.

**Path B — The Gulch.** Tracking Orin (DC 12 Survival) into Bleached Gulch leads to a hidden canyon camp: a bedroll, a half-empty bottle of **Sandtongue Whiskey**, and a journal describing the **Eidolon of Thirst** — a bound water elemental trapped beneath the shrine whose essence Orin plans to siphon into a portable decanter worth a fortune. Two **bandits** and a **dust devil** (reskinned air elemental, CR 2) guard the canyon mouth.

**Path C — Diplomacy.** Persuading Ludo (DC 13 Persuasion) or Tavia (DC 15) reveals that Zahreen had been secretly maintaining the shrine's wards for years. She likely went to confront Orin herself. This path gives the party a **ward-token** (one-use: advantage on saves vs. the Eidolon's abilities) and a shortcut through the aqueduct.

### The Shrine (Climax)

All paths converge at the **Sunken Shrine of Adath-Ka**, a domed chamber carved with wave-patterns now half-choked with sand. Here, **Orin Blacktallow** (use **cult fanatic** stat block, CR 2) has partially unbound the **Eidolon of Thirst** (use **water elemental** with Lair Action: at initiative 20, all water within 30 ft. turns to sand for one round, dealing 2d6 bludgeoning to creatures standing in it, DC 14 Dex save for half).

**Zahreen** is alive but restrained in crystallized sand (DC 15 Athletics or 20 HP to free). She can re-seal the Eidolon if freed — but only if the party holds Orin off for **3 rounds** while she completes the ritual.

### Decision Point at the Climax

- **Seal the Eidolon** (protect Zahreen for 3 rounds): Town saved, Zahreen grateful, full reward.
- **Destroy the Eidolon** (reduce to 0 HP): Water returns but the shrine collapses — the town has maybe five years before the oasis fails permanently. Orin escapes in the chaos.
- **Side with Orin** (help siphon the Eidolon): Party gains a **Decanter of Endless Water** but Sipping Well is doomed within a week. Zahreen becomes a recurring enemy.

### Consequences

If the party seals the Eidolon, dawn breaks with the pools running clear. Tavia pays in full and names them **Wardens of the Well** — a title that grants them free lodging and information in any desert settlement that trades with Sipping Well.
